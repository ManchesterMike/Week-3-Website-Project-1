Koh Chang




Koh Chang is a beautiful island located in the Gulf of Thailand, close to the border with Cambodia. It is the third-largest island in Thailand, after Phuket and Koh Samui, it offers visitors an authentic and laid-back Thai experience. The island is known for its pristine beaches, lush jungles, and crystal-clear waters. It is a popular destination for both locals and tourists who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the big cities and enjoy the peaceful and tranquil atmosphere of the island.


One of the highlights of Koh Chang is its national park, which covers more than 70% of the island. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several species of monkeys, birds, and butterflies. There are also several waterfalls in the park, where visitors can take a refreshing dip in the cool waters. For those who want to explore the island further, there are many hiking trails that lead through the jungle to hidden waterfalls and scenic viewpoints. Koh Chang is also a great place for water sports enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for snorkeling, diving, kayaking, and paddleboarding.


Activities

  • Snorkeling and Diving - Koh Chang is home to some of the most beautiful coral reefs in Thailand, making it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. Visitors can take a day trip to explore the underwater world of Koh Chang or sign up for a multi-day dive trip to explore the surrounding islands.

  • Jungle Trekking - For those who love nature and adventure, jungle trekking is a must-do activity on Koh Chang. Visitors can explore the lush jungle terrain, discover hidden waterfalls, and learn about the island's rich flora and fauna.

  • Island Hopping - Koh Chang is just one of many beautiful islands in the Gulf of Thailand. Visitors can take a day trip to nearby islands like Koh Mak and Koh Kood to explore their unique beauty and charm.











Food In Koh Chang

Koh Chang's cuisine is heavily influenced by traditional Thai cooking with an emphasis on fresh seafood. One of the must-try dishes is the "Tom Yum Goong," which is a spicy and sour soup made with fresh shrimp, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and chili peppers. The seafood in Koh Chang is freshly caught, and you can find everything from squid and crab to lobster and prawns. Visitors can enjoy a range of seafood dishes like grilled fish, stir-fried prawns with garlic, and spicy seafood salads.

Apart from seafood, Koh Chang is also known for its delicious street food. The local night markets offer a range of tasty and affordable dishes such as pad Thai, green curry, and som tam (papaya salad). Another popular dish is the "Kai Jeow," which is a Thai omelet made with vegetables, seafood, or meat. In addition to these traditional Thai dishes, visitors can also find international cuisine, including Italian, Indian, and Japanese.

In summary, Koh Chang's cuisine is a blend of traditional Thai cooking and fresh seafood, offering visitors an exciting culinary experience. With a wide variety of dishes, from street food to high-end restaurants, visitors can enjoy a range of flavors and culinary delights on this tropical island.















How to get to Koh Chang

The easiest way to get to Koh Chang from Bangkok is by bus or private taxi. Buses run regularly from Bangkok's Ekkamai Bus Terminal to Trat, where visitors can take a ferry to Koh Chang. Private taxis can also be arranged from Bangkok, with many tour companies offering door-to-door service to the island.

In conclusion, Koh Chang is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Thailand. With its stunning beaches, diverse activities, and delicious food, it's the perfect spot for a relaxing getaway or adventure-packed holiday.
