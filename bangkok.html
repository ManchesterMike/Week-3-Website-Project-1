Bangkok


Bangkok, the capital city of Thailand, is a vibrant and bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage. From street food to rooftop bars, Bangkok offers an array of experiences for tourists to enjoy. In this blog post, we'll explore three activities to do, details about the food on offer, the top three rooftop bars, and two temples worth visiting.



Activities

  • Explore the Grand Palace - The Grand Palace is a stunning palace complex that was once the residence of the Kings of Siam. The palace is made up of several buildings, including Wat Phra Kaew, the most important Buddhist temple in Thailand. Visitors can marvel at the intricate details of the buildings and the stunning gold decorations that adorn them.

  • Shop at Chatuchak Weekend Market - The Chatuchak Weekend Market is one of the largest outdoor markets in the world, with over 15,000 stalls selling everything from clothing to food. Visitors can wander through the market and experience the lively atmosphere, grab some delicious street food, and pick up some souvenirs to take home.

  • Take a Boat Tour of the Canals - Bangkok is known as the "Venice of the East" due to its extensive network of canals. Taking a boat tour of the canals is a great way to explore the city and see some of its hidden gems. Visitors can see traditional Thai houses, floating markets, and temples along the way.












Food In Bangkok

Bangkok is known for its delicious street food, with countless vendors serving up a variety of dishes at affordable prices. Some of the must-try dishes include Pad Thai, a stir-fried noodle dish with vegetables and peanuts, Tom Yum Goong, a spicy and sour soup with shrimp, and Mango Sticky Rice, a sweet and sticky dessert made with coconut milk and fresh mango. For those looking for a more upscale dining experience, Bangkok is also home to several Michelin-starred restaurants, such as Gaggan and Bo.lan.













Rooftop Bars

Bangkok is known for its vibrant nightlife and stunning skyline, and one of the best ways to enjoy both is by visiting one of the city's many rooftop bars. From the swanky Sky Bar at Lebua State Tower, made famous by the movie "The Hangover Part II," to the trendy Octave Rooftop Lounge & Bar at the Marriott Hotel Sukhumvit, there is no shortage of options to choose from. These rooftop bars offer breathtaking views of the city, delicious cocktails, and a lively atmosphere. They are perfect for a night out with friends, a romantic date, or just soaking up the incredible views of Bangkok's bustling streets and skyscrapers.
